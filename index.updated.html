Job Market Paper

ESG Ratings, Ownership Structure, and Firm Value

This paper examines whether changes in firms’ ESG scores prompt shifts in institutional ownership and whether those shifts affect market valuation. It studies rating upgrades/downgrades, threshold‑crossing events related to ESG index inclusion, and ETF additions/removals. Findings show modest but positive market reactions to rating changes, asymmetric effects on ownership (upgrades ↑, downgrades ↓—especially below index thresholds), and strong negative returns when downgrades are followed by ETF removals. Methods include propensity‑score matching and dynamic difference‑in‑differences analyses.

Teaching Philosophy

“Good teaching is more a giving of right questions than a giving of right answers.” Guided by this view, my classroom emphasizes active learning, question‑driven inquiry, and inclusive collaboration. I design hands‑on activities, peer discussions, and projects that connect finance concepts to both professional and personal decision‑making. I aim to cultivate curiosity, confidence, and perseverance so students become independent problem‑solvers.
