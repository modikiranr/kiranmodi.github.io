About

Kiran Modi is a Ph.D. candidate in Finance at the University of North Texas, specializing in corporate governance, agency conflicts, ownership structure, and ESG integration. Her research examines how institutional ownership and sustainability considerations affect firm value, combining empirical corporate finance with modern data engineering and NLP methods. Research

Research Statement

My research interest is in corporate finance, with a focus on corporate governance, agency conflicts, ownership structure, and how ESG integrates with these broader concepts. These themes guide all of my work, and you can see them in both my completed and ongoing papers.

  • Job Market Paper: Examines the causal effect of ESG ratings on institutional ownership and firm value, using rigorous empirical approaches such as propensity score matching and difference-in-differences to identify the impact of ESG changes on investor behavior and market valuation.
  • Work in Process: Extends these questions to a global setting, comparing how institutional environments across countries influence the link between ownership, ESG, and firm value. This involves large-scale data collection and automation using Python and Morningstar Direct. Another project, Governance by Contract: Shareholder Agreements and Corporate Control, uses SEC contract data and large language models to study how shareholder agreements and legal shocks shape governance outcomes.

Future Directions

Going forward, my research philosophy is to combine rigorous empirical methods with modern technology to explore important questions in corporate finance. I am guided by curiosity and a commitment to hard work, and I hope to continue producing research that is both empirically sound and contributes to a deeper understanding of corporate behavior.
