About

Short Bio

Kiran Modi is a Ph.D. candidate in Finance at the University of North Texas, specializing in corporate governance, agency conflicts, ownership structure, and ESG integration. Her research examines how institutional ownership and sustainability considerations affect firm value, combining empirical corporate finance with modern data engineering and NLP methods. She has received multiple Best Research Paper Awards and Outstanding Student Awards at UNT. Kiran has taught Introduction to Finance and assisted in graduate and undergraduate finance courses, alongside her active research contributions.

Job Market Paper

ESG Ratings, Ownership Structure, and Firm Value

Examines whether changes in ESG ratings drive shifts in institutional ownership and market valuation. Identifies effects around rating upgrades/downgrades, index-threshold crossings, and ETF additions/removals; uses propensity‑score matching and dynamic difference‑in‑differences.
